Access prices fell 69% in three years. Security teams are treating this like good news. It's actually the most dangerous trend in cybersecurity. When criminal services become commodity-priced, the entire threat landscape shifts.

Network access dropped from $1,427 to $439 due to automation, AI tooling, and infostealer oversupply. But commoditization doesn't reduce demand — it explodes volume. DWIQ observed access offers jump from 2,700 to 5,800 in less than a year.

Cheap access enables more threat actors to enter the ecosystem. Lower barriers to entry mean more ransomware operators, more data brokers, more sophisticated monetization platforms.

The security industry optimized defenses for expensive, targeted attacks. We're getting cheap, automated, high-volume exploitation instead.

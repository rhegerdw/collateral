Everyone's focused on preventing ransomware. But the real risk has shifted to what happens after you refuse to pay.

Platforms like Leak Bazaar change the entire calculus of a ransomware incident. Your stolen data doesn't disappear when you refuse to pay — it enters a structured monetization pipeline.

Four-stage processing: automated filtering, database reverse engineering, buyer-oriented categorization, manual validation. Raw dumps become segmented intelligence products sold to multiple buyers over extended periods.

The conventional wisdom says refusing to pay eliminates the threat. That's no longer true.

Post-breach monitoring must account for structured resale, not just raw data dumps on leak sites. The operational life of stolen data now extends well beyond the initial incident.

Your incident response playbook needs updating.

Most ransomware victims aren't paying. But the ones who do are paying record amounts.

Jaguar Land Rover: $2.5 billion in estimated losses. Marks & Spencer: prolonged operational disruption. DaVita: 2.7 million patient records exposed.

These weren't just data breaches — they were business continuity disasters.

Scattered Spider's attack on Marks & Spencer went beyond encryption. Stock prices tumbled. Operations ground to a halt. The reputational damage compounded financial losses.

Interlock's breach of DaVita exposed 2.7 million patient records, triggering regulatory scrutiny and compliance costs that dwarf the original ransom demand.

The $75 million payment to Dark Angels became the largest known ransom in history — a single transaction that reset industry expectations.

While 72% of victims refuse to pay, the outliers who do are funding the entire ecosystem. Each massive payout validates the high-impact, low-probability strategy.

Threat actors are optimizing for home runs, not base hits.

When anyone with $50K can buy enterprise-grade offensive tooling, what happens to the threat landscape?

Distributed brute-force systems with lifetime support. Automated scanning, proxy rotation, web-based dashboards. Targeting every major VPN protocol in one package.

This isn't sophisticated APT infrastructure anymore — it's commercially available software.

Traditional threat modeling assumes skill barriers. Advanced persistent threats require advanced persistent skills. But when offensive capabilities are productized, the barrier drops to purchasing power.

Does this democratize sophisticated attacks? Or create a flood of automated, low-skill operators?

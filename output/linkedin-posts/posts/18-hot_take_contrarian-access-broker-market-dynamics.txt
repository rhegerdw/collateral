The cybersecurity industry celebrates declining ransom payments. 28% in 2025 vs 63% in 2024.

But here's what we're missing: the access broker market is responding with industrialized automation and price deflation.

Network access prices crashed 69% in three years. $50,000 brute-force systems targeting eight VPN protocols are now commercial products with enterprise support.

When turnkey offensive tooling lowers barriers to entry, more actors can generate high-volume access offers. The ransomware supply chain benefits from a deeper, cheaper pool of initial access.

We're winning the payment battle but potentially losing the access war.

The threat landscape is evolving faster than our defensive strategies.

The $75 million payment to Dark Angels tells the complete story of ransomware economics in 2025.

While 72% of victims refused to pay (up from 37% in 2024), those who did pay faced exponentially higher demands. The median payment rose 368% year-over-year.

Jaguar Land Rover suffered $2.5 billion in estimated losses. Scattered Spider's Marks & Spencer breach caused prolonged operational disruption and stock depreciation. Interlock exposed 2.7 million DaVita patient records.

The market bifurcated: most organizations refuse to pay, but critical infrastructure and high-value targets face massive individual payouts.

Threat actors compensated for lower payment rates with exponentially higher demands from the victims who do pay.

Everyone's celebrating the 28% ransomware payment rate like it's a victory. It's not. It's the worst possible outcome for defenders. Lower payment rates don't reduce risk — they multiply it.

When 72% of victims refuse to pay, threat actors don't pack up and go home. They innovate. They build platforms like Leak Bazaar to convert exfiltrated data into structured, sellable intelligence products. They develop industrial-scale automation tools for $50,000 that target eight VPN protocols simultaneously.

The result? More attacks (50% YoY increase), longer data monetization timelines, and sophisticated resale mechanisms that extend breach impact for months or years.

Refusing to pay used to end the threat. Now it just shifts the monetization strategy. Organizations thinking they've "won" by not paying are discovering their data has a much longer criminal shelf life than anticipated.

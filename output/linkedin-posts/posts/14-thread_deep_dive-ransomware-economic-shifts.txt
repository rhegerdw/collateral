Three data points tell the story of ransomware's economic evolution in 2025: 28% payment rate. 8,000+ victims named on leak sites. $75 million single ransom payment.

→ THE VOLUME PARADOX
Attacks increased 50% year-over-year despite historic low payment rates. Total on-chain payments dropped to $820 million (Chainalysis). More attacks, fewer successful extortions, yet the ecosystem expanded.

→ THE PAYMENT BIFURCATION
While overall payment rates collapsed from 63% to 28%, median ransom payments rose 368% year-over-year. The Dark Angels group's $75 million payday illustrates the new dynamic: fewer victims pay, but those who do often pay substantially more.

→ THE MONETIZATION GAP
With 72% of victims refusing to pay, threat actors faced a growing inventory of exfiltrated data with no traditional monetization path. This drove innovation in alternative revenue streams: structured data processing, segmented purchasing, analytical reports.

→ THE INFRASTRUCTURE RESPONSE
Platforms like Leak Bazaar emerged to address the monetization gap. Instead of binary extortion, stolen data becomes a refinable asset with extended operational life through structured resale.

The result: organizations can no longer eliminate risk by refusing to pay. The threat calculus fundamentally changed in 2025.

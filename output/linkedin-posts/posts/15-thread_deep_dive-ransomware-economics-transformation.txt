The ransomware economy underwent fundamental transformation in 2025. Payment rates collapsed while attack volume exploded. Here's the data breakdown:

→ PAYMENT RATE COLLAPSE
28% of victims paid in 2025 (down from 63% in 2024, 79% in 2022). Total on-chain payments fell to $820 million despite record activity.

→ ATTACK VOLUME SURGE
50% year-over-year increase in claimed attacks. 8,000+ organizations publicly named on leak sites — the most active year on record.

→ ACCESS PRICE DEFLATION
Network access prices crashed 69%: $1,427 in Q1 2023 → $439 in Q1 2026. Driven by automation, AI-assisted tooling, oversupply from infostealer logs.

→ PAYMENT BIFURCATION
While fewer victims pay, those who do face exponentially higher demands. Median payment rose 368% year-over-year. Single largest payment: $75 million to Dark Angels.

→ MONETIZATION GAP
Growing inventory of exfiltrated data from non-paying victims creates new market opportunity: structured data monetization platforms like Leak Bazaar.

The combined effect: threat actors are seeking alternative revenue streams from stolen data when traditional extortion fails.

This shift from binary extortion to asset monetization fundamentally changes post-breach risk calculations.

If only 28% of victims pay ransoms, but those who do pay 368% more than before, what's the optimal strategy?

Threat actors are optimizing for massive payouts from the minority who can't afford downtime. The $75M payment to Dark Angels proved the high-stakes model works.

But it creates a negotiation paradox: operators demand astronomical amounts because they know most victims won't pay anything.

How do you negotiate with adversaries whose business model assumes you'll probably walk away?

The median payment surge suggests successful negotiations involve organizations with existential operational dependencies — those who literally cannot function offline.

Are we inadvertently training threat actors to target our most critical infrastructure first?

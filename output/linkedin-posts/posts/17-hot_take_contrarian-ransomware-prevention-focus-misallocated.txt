Everyone talks about ransomware prevention. But the real risk has shifted to post-exfiltration data monetization.

We're solving yesterday's problem.

Prevention assumes binary outcomes: either you get encrypted and pay, or you don't. But platforms like Leak Bazaar turn refused ransoms into structured intelligence products sold to multiple buyers over extended periods.

Your financial reports, M&A plans, R&D materials — refined, categorized, resold. The operational life of stolen data now extends far beyond initial encryption.

While security teams optimize for preventing initial compromise, threat actors are building revenue pipelines that function whether you pay or not.

The new reality: successful prevention still leaves you exposed if exfiltration occurred. Post-breach monitoring must account for structured resale across specialized platforms, not just raw dumps on leak sites.

We need to shift budget from preventing every breach to limiting data value when breaches occur.

Data classification, segmentation, and post-incident monitoring matter more than perfect prevention.

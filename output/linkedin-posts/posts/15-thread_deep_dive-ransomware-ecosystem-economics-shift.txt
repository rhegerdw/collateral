The ransomware economy isn't collapsing — it's restructuring.

Payment rates dropped 55%, but attack volume surged 50%. Here's how the math works:

→ 2024 BASELINE
63% payment rate, moderate attack volume, $1.4B+ total payments

→ 2025 REALITY
28% payment rate (-55%), 50% more attacks, $820M total payments (-35%)

→ THE GAP PROBLEM
More attacks, fewer paying victims = massive inventory of monetized exfiltrated data. Traditional extortion model breaks down when 72% refuse to pay.

→ ECOSYSTEM ADAPTATION
Access prices crashed 69% due to oversupply from automated tooling. Premium access (domain admin, cloud consoles) still commands thousands.

→ REVENUE DIVERSIFICATION
Platforms like Leak Bazaar emerge to monetize non-paying victim data through structured resale. Transform binary extortion into ongoing asset monetization.

→ PAYMENT POLARIZATION
Median ransom up 368%. $75M single payment to Dark Angels. Fewer victims pay, but those who do pay exponentially more.

Result: Volume-based ecosystem with multiple revenue streams. Attack frequency increases, monetization diversifies, high-value targets pay record amounts.

Threat actors aren't abandoning ransomware — they're industrializing it.

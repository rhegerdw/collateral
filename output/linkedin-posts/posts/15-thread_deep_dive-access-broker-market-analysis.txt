The initial access broker market underwent fundamental transformation in 2025-2026. Price deflation, volume expansion, and industrial-scale automation redefined the economics of criminal access.

→ PRICE COLLAPSE
DWIQ operational data shows average network access prices fell from $1,427 in Q1 2023 to $439 in Q1 2026 — a 69% deflation driven by automation, AI-assisted tooling, and infostealer log oversupply.

→ MARKET SEGMENTATION
While bulk access became commodity-priced, premium access types maintained high valuations: domain administrator credentials, Citrix environments with full internal reach, and cloud management consoles still command thousands to tens of thousands of dollars.

→ VOLUME EXPLOSION
DWIQ observed approximately 5,800 access offers in Q3 2025, up from roughly 2,700 in late 2024. The proliferation of automated tooling lowered technical barriers for individual operators to generate high-volume access offers.

→ INDUSTRIAL TOOLING
The $50,000 distributed brute-force system targeting 8 enterprise VPN protocols represents complete market maturation: enterprise pricing, lifetime support, custom development, professional documentation. Criminal tooling adopted legitimate software practices.

→ PROTOCOL TARGETING
Fortinet, SonicWall, and Cisco VPN products consistently dominated access-type distributions throughout 2025. The industrial tooling specifically targets these platforms alongside five additional protocols in unified attack pipelines.

This transformation enabled the ransomware volume increases observed in 2025 while simultaneously contributing to payment rate decline through access commoditization.

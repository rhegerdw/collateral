When 72% of ransomware victims refuse to pay, what happens to all that exfiltrated data?

It doesn't just disappear. Threat actors are building sophisticated platforms to convert raw dumps into structured, sellable intelligence products. Automated filtering, database reverse engineering, categorized purchasing, manual validation.

Leak Bazaar represents a fundamental shift: from data as extortion leverage to data as refinable asset. Organizations can no longer assume that refusing to pay eliminates the value of stolen information.

How should this change your post-breach monitoring strategy?

Leak Bazaar isn't just another leak site.

It's a four-stage data refinement pipeline that transforms raw exfiltrated corporate data into structured intelligence products. Here's how it works:

→ AUTOMATED FILTERING
ML-assisted removal of system debris (OS backups, DLLs, ISOs) combined with NLP-based text analysis of document arrays.

→ DATABASE REVERSE ENGINEERING
Server-side parsing of raw SQL, SAP, and Oracle exports into structured Excel/CSV outputs containing financial transactions, payroll records, counterparty data. Currently in beta.

→ CATEGORIZATION
Processed data segmented into buyer-oriented categories: quarterly financial reports, M&A data, R&D material including source code, and personal data.

→ MANUAL VALIDATION
In-house analyst review before listing.

The platform generates structured analytical reports identifying OFAC/SDN violations, shadow accounting, unpublished financial results — negotiation pressure tools replacing crude file listings.

Buyers can purchase specific categories rather than entire dumps. Two models: exclusive (full price, data removed after sale) or shared (lower price, remains available for resale).

Revenue split: 70% supplier, 30% platform.

For organizations, this changes everything. Refusing to pay no longer eliminates data value — it triggers structured monetization.

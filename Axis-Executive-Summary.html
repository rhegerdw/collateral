What We Found

DarkWebIQ analysts ran the AXIS 100 portfolio against our proprietary intelligence holdings — including Infostealer bot logs and Initial Access Broker victim data obtained through direct, one-on-one engagement with threat actors operating inside criminal networks.

Across an 18-month observation window, nearly one in four portfolio companies showed active compromise telemetry. The 23 affected entities generated a combined 1,158 individual Infostealer log entries and 12 confirmed IAB listings, with three companies exhibiting the highest-risk pattern: overlapping Infostealer infections and confirmed network access sales.

This is not theoretical exposure. These are vetted signals of credentials already harvested by malware and, in the most critical cases, network access already listed for sale to ransomware operators.
